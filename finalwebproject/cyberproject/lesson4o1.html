DDoS & Email Flooding Attacks

Understanding how availability‑based attacks disrupt systems and communication

Distributed Denial‑of‑Service (DDoS)

A distributed denial‑of‑service (DDoS) attack aims to make a service unavailable by overwhelming it with traffic from many sources simultaneously. Botnets are especially effective for DDoS because they generate traffic from thousands of legitimate and geographically distributed devices.

  • Traffic floods overwhelm servers and network links
  • Residential IP addresses make blocking difficult
  • Attacks can be repeated, timed, or sustained

Email Flooding Attacks

Email flooding is a form of availability attack targeting inboxes rather than web services. Attackers generate massive volumes of email to prevent victims from accessing legitimate communication.

  • Inbox saturation prevents normal use
  • Important messages may be missed
  • Often combined with harassment or pressure campaigns

Role of Automation

Automation allows attackers to conduct both DDoS and email flooding at scale. Once attack systems are configured, they can operate continuously with minimal human interaction, greatly increasing impact.

Impact of These Attacks

Availability attacks affect more than systems. They can disrupt businesses, research, emergency communication, and personal safety, making them a serious cybersecurity concern even without data theft.

Defensive Awareness

Understanding how these attacks work helps defenders prepare mitigations, recognize attack patterns early, and reduce operational and human impact.
