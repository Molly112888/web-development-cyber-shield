DDoS & Email Flooding Attacks

Understanding how availability‑based attacks disrupt systems and communication

Distributed Denial‑of‑Service (DDoS)

DDoS attacks aim to make a service unavailable by overwhelming it with traffic from many sources. Botnets are especially effective because they generate traffic from thousands of legitimate, geographically spread devices.

  • Traffic floods overwhelm servers and network links
  • Residential IP addresses make blocking difficult
  • Attacks can be repeated, timed, or sustained

Email Flooding Attacks

Email flooding targets inboxes instead of web services. Attackers send massive volumes of email so victims struggle to access legitimate communication.

  • Inbox saturation prevents normal use
  • Important messages may be missed
  • Often combined with harassment or pressure campaigns

Role of Automation

Automation lets attackers scale both DDoS and email flooding. Once systems are set up, they can run continuously with little human input, amplifying damage.

Impact of These Attacks

Availability attacks disrupt more than systems. They can affect businesses, research, emergency communication, and personal safety — serious concerns even without data theft.

Defensive Awareness

Knowing how these attacks work helps defenders prepare mitigations, spot attack patterns early, and reduce both operational and human impact.
