Botnet Infrastructure

Understanding how botnets are structured, controlled, and scaled

What Is Botnet Infrastructure?

Botnet infrastructure is the technical framework that lets attackers control large numbers of compromised devices at once. This structure enables coordination, persistence, and multiple attack types through a single distributed system.

Command and Control (C2)

C2 systems issue instructions to infected devices. Attackers use them to start or stop attacks, update malware, or redirect the botnet toward new targets.

  • Centralized C2 servers controlling all bots
  • Decentralized or peer‑to‑peer botnet designs
  • Encrypted communication to evade detection

Bots (Infected Devices)

Bots are compromised machines acting on behalf of attackers. Modern botnets often exploit consumer and IoT devices because of weak security and limited monitoring.

  • Home routers and IoT devices
  • Smart TVs, cameras, and digital appliances
  • Poorly maintained or outdated systems

Communication Channels

Bots need regular instructions from attackers. This communication is often disguised to look like normal traffic.

  • Web traffic using HTTP or HTTPS
  • DNS‑based signaling
  • Proxy networks and encrypted tunnels

Why Infrastructure Matters for Defense

Understanding botnet infrastructure helps defenders spot threats early. Disrupting command channels, isolating infected devices, or reducing internal trust can greatly weaken a botnet.
