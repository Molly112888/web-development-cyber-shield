Botnet Infrastructure

Understanding how botnets are structured, controlled, and scaled

What Is Botnet Infrastructure?

Botnet infrastructure refers to the technical framework that allows attackers to control large numbers of compromised devices at the same time. This structure enables coordination, persistence, and the execution of multiple attack types using a single distributed system.

Command and Control (C2)

Command‑and‑control systems are responsible for issuing instructions to infected devices. Through C2, attackers can start or stop attacks, update malware, or redirect the botnet toward new targets.

  • Centralized C2 servers controlling all bots
  • Decentralized or peer‑to‑peer botnet designs
  • Encrypted communication to evade detection

Bots (Infected Devices)

Bots are compromised machines that perform actions on behalf of the attacker. Modern botnets often rely on consumer and IoT devices due to weak security and limited monitoring.

  • Home routers and IoT devices
  • Smart TVs, cameras, and digital appliances
  • Poorly maintained or outdated systems

Communication Channels

Bots must regularly receive instructions from the attacker. This communication is often disguised to blend into normal network traffic.

  • Web traffic using HTTP or HTTPS
  • DNS‑based signaling
  • Proxy networks and encrypted tunnels

Why Infrastructure Matters for Defense

Understanding botnet infrastructure helps defenders identify threats early. Disrupting command channels, isolating infected devices, or reducing internal trust can significantly limit botnet effectiveness.
