Cyber Attacks & Ransomware Awareness

Cybercrime is one of the fastest‑growing threats in the digital world. Every year, organizations, governments, and individuals fall victim to ransomware attacks, data breaches, and cyber extortion.

This website is designed to help learners understand how modern cyber attacks work, why ransomware is so dangerous, and how cybercriminal groups operate behind the scenes.

What Is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicious software that locks or encrypts computer systems and demands payment—usually in cryptocurrency—to restore access. In many cases, attackers also steal sensitive data and threaten to publish it.

Modern Cyber Attack Techniques

  • Ransomware and double extortion attacks
  • Credential theft and unauthorized system access
  • Data exfiltration and data leaks
  • Supply‑chain attacks affecting multiple organizations
  • Cryptocurrency‑based extortion and laundering

Why These Lessons Matter

Real‑world ransomware attacks have caused millions of euros in economic damage, disrupted essential services, and exposed sensitive personal and business data. Understanding how these attacks happen is the first step toward prevention.

Throughout this lesson series, real investigations are used only as examples to explain attack methods, criminal strategies, and law‑enforcement responses—not to focus on individuals.

What You Will Learn

  • How ransomware attacks begin and spread
  • What “double extortion” means
  • How cybercriminal groups organize themselves
  • The role of cryptocurrency in cybercrime
  • How attacks are investigated and disrupted
  • How individuals and organizations can reduce risk
