Modern Ransomware Explained

Modern ransomware attacks are highly organized cybercrimes. They rely on advanced tactics and teamwork to force victims into paying large sums of money. Two key ideas define how these attacks work: double extortion and organized cybercriminal groups.

What Does “Double Extortion” Mean?

Double extortion is a ransomware technique where attackers demand payment in two different ways instead of one.

Step 1: Attackers encrypt the victim’s systems and files.

Step 2: Sensitive data is stolen before encryption.

Victims are forced to pay twice to recover systems and protect data.

How Cybercriminal Groups Organize Themselves

Ransomware groups operate like businesses using Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS).

Developers: Create ransomware tools.

Affiliates: Carry out attacks.

Access Brokers: Sell stolen credentials.

Launderers: Obscure ransom payments.

Key Takeaways

Double extortion targets systems and sensitive data.

Ransomware groups function like businesses.

Cybercrime depends on teamwork and profit sharing.
